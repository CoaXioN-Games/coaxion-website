Hi everybody, Tholp here, the Technical and Programming Lead of DMC:R; I’ve got a couple of the more visual things I’ve been working on to show you.

Create Server Menu

First, we have our new create server menu. The main goal with this was to make it quick to find the map you wanted and the options that are relevant. Maps can be filtered by playable gamemodes and by search text. Each map can set a recommended player count and include a description for lore or just info about the map. Everything still looks a bit bland or has programmer art, as we’re still trying to figure out how we want our menus to be styled.

Marker System

Next, we now have a few different types of location and respawn indicators for most of our pickups and objectives. (That is: weapons, megahealth, armor, powerups and flags.) More important items like flags and powerups will be visible at certain times through walls.

With this, we also now have a feature where if you try to select a weapon using a keybind, the locations of spawners with that weapon will be shown to you, or you can press Z to show every weapon! This can be turned off on a per spawner basis for items intended to be secret.

Map makers will also be able to leverage this system and place markers around their maps and control their state through hammer logic, we hope to see this used in combination with our custom gamemode system when the game releases!

Player Shading

Maps tend to have pretty dark theming so making sure players stay visible is important to keep things fun. The original DMC simply scaled up the player’s received ambient lighting by a factor of 100, and it didn’t look that good even back then.

Now, players are affected normally by map lighting, but have colored rimlights when it gets too dark. The specifics of how this looks might change in the future but we are happy with this for now. Now, back to MTD.

And that’ll mostly do it for this devlog, but I want to discuss one more important thing...

With most of the game and its planned content being in a playable state, this begs the question of why we haven’t made the Steam page publicly visible yet.

For the most part, it comes down to many of the assets in our game still being placeholders from other games or just not having textures ready. Pretty much right after our previous devlog was published, our only texture artist had to step back from the project. With them gone, we weren’t able to fully finish any more of our new assets. Even now, a lot of our assets have gotten remade but are still textureless.

We know we can always display messages stating how much of a work in progress the game is, but with our assets, I still don’t feel comfortable enough to consider them ready to be displayed on a public steam page.

On the bright side, we were able to bring a new texture artist onto the team, Kalin. They’ve textured our new Machinegun, Super Shotgun and Armor models, so the lack of textured models shouldn’t be a problem for too much longer.

Regardless, We’re still always looking for help in all fields, whether that be modeling, texturing, programming, particle effects, etc. So if our project seems worth contributing to you, feel free to hop in our Discord server and send us a message!

That’ll be it for this devlog. We hope you enjoyed what we had to show in this new format and I most definitely hope the next one doesn't take two years. But the jury’s still out on that. See ya!
